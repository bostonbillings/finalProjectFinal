Skiing in Utah is amazing because it offers some of the best snow on Earth, with its famous "Greatest Snow on Earth" tagline thanks to the dry, powdery conditions created by Utah's unique climate. The state boasts world-class ski resorts like Park City, Alta, and Snowbird, which cater to all skill levels, from beginners to seasoned experts. With breathtaking mountain landscapes, over 500 inches of snowfall annually, and easy access to resorts within an hour of Salt Lake City, Utah provides unparalleled convenience and beauty. Whether you're carving through fresh powder or enjoying the vibrant après-ski scene, skiing in Utah is an unforgettable experience for all winter sports enthusiasts.

Check out these popular ski resorts in Utah plus my rating from 1-7, 1 being the highest!

  1. Snowbasin

    2. Snowbasin Ski Resort, nestled in the picturesque Wasatch Mountains near Ogden, Utah, is a world-renowned destination that offers an exceptional skiing and snowboarding experience. Known for its blend of natural beauty and luxury, the resort features over 3,000 acres of diverse terrain, catering to everyone from first-time skiers to seasoned experts. Snowbasin is famous for its wide-open bowls, challenging moguls, pristine groomed trails, and breathtaking views at every turn. It also boasts a rich history, having hosted the downhill, super-G, and combined events during the 2002 Winter Olympics, solidifying its reputation as a world-class facility. Visitors enjoy high-speed gondolas and chairlifts, ensuring quick access to the slopes, as well as opulent lodges like Earl’s Lodge, known for its elegant design and gourmet dining. With an average annual snowfall of over 300 inches and one of the best snowmaking systems in the industry, Snowbasin guarantees excellent conditions throughout the season. Whether you're there for the thrill of the slopes or the relaxing après-ski atmosphere, Snowbasin combines adventure and comfort, making it a must-visit resort for winter sports enthusiasts.
    • My Score: 1

  2. Snowbird

    3. Snowbird Ski Resort, located in Utah’s stunning Little Cottonwood Canyon, is a paradise for skiers and snowboarders seeking thrilling terrain and breathtaking alpine scenery. Known for its legendary powder, Snowbird receives an impressive average of 500 inches of snow annually, creating ideal conditions for winter sports enthusiasts. The resort spans over 2,500 acres, offering a mix of terrain that caters to all skill levels, from beginner-friendly runs to expert-level chutes, bowls, and tree skiing. Snowbird is famous for its iconic Aerial Tram, which whisks visitors 2,900 vertical feet to Hidden Peak, where they are greeted with panoramic views and unparalleled access to the mountain’s most challenging runs. As one of the premier destinations for advanced skiers and riders, Snowbird’s reputation for deep snow and rugged terrain is complemented by modern amenities, including luxurious lodges, diverse dining options, and rejuvenating spa facilities. Its close proximity to Salt Lake City makes it incredibly accessible, making Snowbird an ideal spot for anyone looking to experience Utah’s incredible winter magic.
    • My Score: 7

  3. Solitude

    4. Solitude Mountain Resort, tucked away in Utah’s serene Big Cottonwood Canyon, offers a peaceful and authentic skiing experience for those looking to escape the crowds. With over 1,200 acres of skiable terrain and an average annual snowfall of 500 inches, Solitude is a hidden gem that caters to all ability levels. Beginners and families can enjoy wide, groomed trails, while advanced skiers and snowboarders can tackle steep chutes, tree runs, and challenging bowls. The resort's charming village atmosphere, complete with cozy lodges and inviting restaurants, creates a welcoming and intimate environment. Unlike larger resorts, Solitude lives up to its name, providing short lift lines and uncrowded slopes, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the natural beauty of the Wasatch Mountains. Whether you're seeking thrilling runs or a laid-back mountain retreat, Solitude offers a perfect blend of adventure and tranquility, making it a favorite among locals and travelers alike.
    • My Score: 4

  4. Park City

    5. Park City Mountain Resort, located in the heart of Park City, Utah, is one of the largest and most iconic ski destinations in the United States. Spanning over 7,300 acres of skiable terrain, the resort offers an incredible variety of runs, from gentle groomers for beginners to challenging steeps and terrain parks for advanced skiers and snowboarders. Its seamless connection to the historic town of Park City provides a unique blend of world-class skiing and vibrant après-ski culture, with easy access to fine dining, boutique shopping, and lively nightlife. Park City Mountain Resort also boasts state-of-the-art facilities, including high-speed lifts and gondolas that efficiently whisk visitors up the slopes. With its rich history, including hosting events during the 2002 Winter Olympics, and an average annual snowfall of 355 inches, Park City Mountain Resort delivers a premier winter experience that combines adventure, luxury, and charm in one unforgettable destination.
    • My Score: 2

  5. Alta

    6. Alta Ski Area, located in Utah’s breathtaking Little Cottonwood Canyon, is a legendary destination for skiers seeking unparalleled powder and a pure, traditional skiing experience. With an average of 540 inches of snow annually, Alta is renowned for its light, fluffy powder and challenging terrain that attracts skiers from around the world. Spanning over 2,600 acres, the resort offers everything from gentle beginner slopes to steep chutes, open bowls, and thrilling tree runs, making it a haven for advanced and expert skiers. Alta is one of the few ski-only resorts in the country, preserving a classic skiing atmosphere free from snowboarding. Its welcoming, no-frills vibe, combined with stunning mountain vistas and a rich history dating back to 1938, creates an authentic alpine experience. Whether you're carving fresh tracks in untouched powder or enjoying the peaceful beauty of the canyon, Alta delivers a unique blend of world-class skiing and timeless mountain charm.
    • My Score: 3

  6. Sundance

    7. Sundance Mountain Resort, nestled at the base of Utah’s majestic Mount Timpanogos, is a unique destination that combines world-class skiing with a rich focus on art, culture, and sustainability. Spanning 500 acres of skiable terrain, the resort offers a mix of gentle runs for beginners, scenic groomed trails for intermediates, and challenging steeps for advanced skiers and snowboarders. Sundance receives an average annual snowfall of 300 inches, ensuring great conditions throughout the season. Beyond the slopes, the resort is renowned for its intimate, rustic charm, with eco-friendly practices and an emphasis on preserving the surrounding natural beauty. Sundance’s cozy lodges, art studio workshops, and the famous Sundance Film Festival bring a creative and vibrant energy to the mountain. Whether you’re carving through pristine powder, taking in the stunning alpine views, or enjoying a gourmet meal by a roaring fire, Sundance offers an unforgettable mountain escape that blends adventure and artistry.
    • My Score: 5

  7. Brighton

    8. Brighton Resort, located in Utah’s picturesque Big Cottonwood Canyon, is a beloved destination known for its laid-back atmosphere, incredible snowfall, and family-friendly vibe. Receiving an impressive average of 500 inches of snow annually, Brighton boasts consistently excellent conditions for skiers and snowboarders alike. The resort spans over 1,000 acres of diverse terrain, featuring groomed runs for beginners, wide-open bowls for intermediates, and challenging tree runs and steeps for advanced riders. Brighton is also famous for its affordability and free skiing for children 10 and under, making it a top choice for families. With every lift providing access to the entire mountain, exploring Brighton is straightforward and fun, and its well-lit night skiing adds extra excitement to the experience. Surrounded by stunning alpine scenery and offering a friendly, welcoming environment, Brighton is a quintessential Utah resort that guarantees great snow, great vibes, and unforgettable memories.
    • My Score: 6
